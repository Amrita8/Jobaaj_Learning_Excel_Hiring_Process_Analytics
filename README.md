# Hiring Process Analytics

## 📌 Project Overview

The hiring process is a fundamental function of any organization. This project analyzes key recruitment trends—including the number of interviews, rejections, job types, and vacancies—to provide the hiring department with actionable business insights.

## 📊 Project Objectives

* Thoroughly analyze recruitment data to understand core business problems.


* Identify hiring patterns based on gender and departmental needs.


* Evaluate salary benchmarks and distribution across various roles.


* Assess the distribution of candidates across different position tiers.



## 🛠️ Tech Stack

* 
**MS Excel**: Used for data exploration, cleaning, complex analysis, and visualization.


* 
**MS PowerPoint**: Used for final reporting and presentation of insights.



## 🗂️ Data Preprocessing

Before analysis, the following data-cleaning steps were performed to ensure accuracy:

* 
**Handling Missing Data**: Identified missing values in `event_name` (15) and `offered salary` (1). These blanks were dropped to maintain data integrity.


* 
**Outlier Detection**: Conducted a check for outliers that could skew statistical results. Three outliers were identified and deleted during the salary distribution analysis.



## 📈 Key Insights & Analysis

### 1. Hiring Analysis

* 
**Total Hires**: A total of **4,687** applicants were successfully hired.


* 
**Gender Breakdown**: The hired workforce consists of **2,563** males, **1,856** females, and a remaining group who preferred not to disclose their gender.



### 2. Salary & Distribution Analysis

* 
**Average Salary**: The average offered salary across the organization is **49,991.097**.


* 
**Salary Intervals**: Salaries were analyzed using class intervals to understand the frequency of different pay ranges.



### 3. Departmental Performance

* 
**Operations Department**: Recorded the highest volume of successful hires with **1,840** individuals.


* 
**HR Department**: Recorded the lowest volume of hires at **70** individuals.



### 4. Position Tier Analysis

* The project tracked the number of individuals assigned to various post names (e.g., c5, c9, i7) to evaluate staffing levels across different tiers.
